Headline finding
----------------

`qg_recompute_deg` accounts for **95.1 %** of total wall time on
bcsstk14.  Day 4's supervariable detection (the suspect named in
`SPRINT_23/bench_summary_day12.md "(b)"`) contributes only **1.0 %**
across the per-pivot calls plus the **0.0 %** initial all-vertices
scan — total Day-4 cost is ~42.5 ms vs the 4 114 ms degree-recompute
cost.

This contradicts the Sprint 23 retrospective hypothesis that the
O(k²) supervariable hash compare was the bottleneck.  The actual
bottleneck is `qg_compute_deg_exact`'s element-side walk: it expands
each element in u's element-side to its full variable-set and dedupes
across elements via `deg_mark[]`.  Sprint 22's pre-Day-3 quotient
graph had no elements, so this walk didn't exist; Day 3's element
absorption introduced elements, and Day 4's exact-degree formula
(retained as default; Day 5's approximate-degree is opt-in) walks
the new element side.

The cost is fundamentally O(adjacency-of-adjacency) per recompute_deg
call: for each vertex u touched by a pivot, we walk u's elements
(elen[u]) and sum their variable-sets.  On bcsstk14 with high
element-side overlap, this scales poorly — Day 12's bcsstk14 measure
of 6 951 ms is consistent with the 4 325 ms here (the 60 % gap is
run-to-run host-load noise).

Fix-candidate evaluation
------------------------

The original three candidates from `bench_summary_day12.md "(b)"`
target Day 4's supervariable detection.  The profile shows that's
not the bottleneck:

- **(a) sorted-list compare in supervariable detection** — would
  speed up at most 41.937 ms (1 % of total).  Best case bcsstk14
  drops from 4 325 ms to 4 283 ms — far short of the 1.5× Sprint
  22 baseline target (~210 ms).  Insufficient.
- **(b) regularity-heuristic gating** — same ceiling: ~1 % savings.
  Insufficient.
- **(c) revert Days 2-5 entirely** — restores Sprint 22's
  variable-only quotient graph (no elements ⇒ no element-side
  walk in deg_exact ⇒ no per-pivot O(adj-of-adj) cost).  Sprint
  22's qg-AMD on bcsstk14 was ~140 ms.  This is the fix the
  profile points at directly.

A new candidate the profile suggests:

- **(d) optimise `qg_compute_deg_exact`'s element-side walk** —
  e.g. cache element variable-sets, dedup at element level rather
  than vertex level, or skip elements whose variable-set is a
  subset of an already-walked element.  Possible but unproven;
  Sprint 22's variable-only design avoided this complexity entirely.

Day 1 picks **candidate (c) revert Days 2-5**.  See
`fix_decision_day1.md` for the full rationale + implementation plan.
