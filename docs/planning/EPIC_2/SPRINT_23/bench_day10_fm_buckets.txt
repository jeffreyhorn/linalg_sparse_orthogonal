Sprint 23 Day 10 — Pres_Poisson wall-time after FM gain-bucket swap
===================================================================

Day 10 swaps `graph_refine_fm`'s O(n) max-gain scan for the Day-9
gain-bucket structure (see `src/sparse_graph_fm_buckets.h`).  This
captures Pres_Poisson's reorder wall-time and fill quality under
the new FM, comparing against Sprint 22 Day 14 and Day 8.

Single run on macOS x86_64 (Apple Clang, -O2).
Driver: `build/bench_reorder --only Pres_Poisson --skip-factor`.

Fill-quality finding
--------------------

ND nnz(L) drops from 2 839 346 (Day 8) to 2 737 253 (Day 10) — a
3.6 % reduction.  This is the bucket-FM doing more iterations of
gain-update per pass within the same wall-clock budget; Sprint 22's
O(n²) FM ran the same number of passes but each pass picked the
max-gain via a linear scan, so under the same per-level FM budget
the gain-bucket version converges to a tighter cut on the
Pres_Poisson 2D-PDE separator structure.

Even relative to Sprint 22's pre-Day-7 baseline (1.063×), Day 10
shaves another 3.5 percentage points off the ND/AMD ratio (1.063 →
1.026).

The Day-7 leaf-AMD splice contributed the smaller fixtures' wins
(nos4 -11 % on Day 8); Day 10's bucket-FM contributes the
Pres_Poisson-class large-fixture win.

Wall-time finding (vs PLAN.md target ≤ 10 s)
--------------------------------------------

ND reorder_ms is 43 338.9 ms — within run-to-run noise of Sprint 22
Day 14's 43 754.9 ms.  The plan's expectation of ≤ 10 s under the
gain-bucket swap is *not* met.

Why the wall time didn't drop more:

  - The bucket-FM removes Sprint 22's O(n²) max-gain scan, but the
    FM itself is not the dominant cost in `sparse_reorder_nd` on
    Pres_Poisson.  Single-shot `sparse_graph_partition` (the full
    multilevel coarsen + bisect + FM-uncoarsen pipeline) on
    Pres_Poisson now runs in ~200 ms (was ~280 ms pre-Day-10) per
    `test_partition_pres_poisson_smoke`.  Recursive ND makes O(log
    n) of these calls cumulatively totalling ~10-15 s.

  - The remaining ~30 s is split between Day 7's per-leaf AMD splice
    (Sprint 23 qg-AMD has its own ~6-77× wall-time regression vs
    Sprint 22 baseline — see `bench_day8_nd_leaf_amd.txt` "AMD
    wall-time regression" section, filed for Day 12 investigation),
    graph subgraph extraction, and the per-recursion-level
    vertex-id-map splicing.

  - Fixing Day 12's AMD wall-time regression would carry the bulk
    of the closure to ≤ 10 s; the bucket-FM swap landed the FM-side
    contribution.  Plan target deviation routed there rather than
    leaving Day 10 with a misleading completion claim.

bcsstk04 LDL^T residual hazard (resolved during Day 10)
-------------------------------------------------------

The first bucket-FM iteration's tie-breaking + balance-skip
semantics produced a permutation that crashed
`test_ldlt_via_nd_dispatch`'s no-pivoting LDL^T factorization on
bcsstk04 to a 7.94 e+09 residual.  Root cause: balance-ineligible
vertices were dropped from the pop sequence permanently, where
Sprint 22's full-rescan retried them on subsequent steps after
w0/w1 shifted.  The skipped-this-step deferred-reinsertion pattern
documented inline (search "skipped_this_step" in
`src/sparse_graph.c`) restores Sprint 22's "consider every unlocked
vertex every step" semantics and brings the residual back to
6.02 e-12 (bit-identical to the Sprint 22 baseline).

This is the contract the plan's "All `test_partition_*` tests pass
with bit-identical or improved separator sizes vs Sprint-22
baseline" criterion didn't capture — the bcsstk04+LDL^T+no-pivoting
test is a *derived* numerical contract that the FM swap had to
preserve.  Test landed Sprint 22 Day 8; pinned Day 10 onwards.

Day-10 verdict
--------------

Bucket-FM swap landed: cleanly replaces the O(n) max-gain scan
with O(1) amortised pop_max.  Fill quality on Pres_Poisson improved
3.7 pp (ND/AMD: 1.064× → 1.026×); 10×10 grid improved 0.4 pp
(1.20× → 1.16× per `tests/test_reorder_nd.c` output).  Wall-time
dropped from Day 8's 47.6 s to 43.3 s — a 9 % improvement that
returns Sprint 22's baseline; the plan's ≤ 10 s target requires
unblocking the AMD wall-time regression that's deferred to Day 12.

All 39 `tests/test_graph.c` partition tests pass; all 12
`tests/test_reorder_nd.c` tests pass (including the bcsstk04 LDL^T
residual that crashed in the initial bucket-FM iteration); all 5
`tests/test_graph_fm_buckets.c` tests still pass on the now-
production-coupled bucket structure.
