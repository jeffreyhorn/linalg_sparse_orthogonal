Sprint 20 Day 6 — transparent LDL^T dispatch: per-matrix chosen
path, factor / solve timings via `sparse_ldlt_factor_opts` with
`SPARSE_LDLT_BACKEND_AUTO`, and comparison against
`SPARSE_LDLT_BACKEND_LINKED_LIST` forced on the same corpus.

Context
-------
Sprint 20 Day 5 wired `sparse_ldlt_factor_opts` to dispatch between
the linked-list LDL^T kernel and the CSC supernodal pipeline based
on `opts->backend`.  AUTO routes CSC when
`n >= SPARSE_CSC_THRESHOLD` (default 100) and linked-list below.
Day 6 adds a `--dispatch` mode to `bench_ldlt_csc` that factors
each matrix under AUTO and under forced LINKED_LIST for direct
comparison.  CSV columns:

  matrix, n, nnz, backend,
  factor_auto_ms, factor_ll_ms,
  solve_auto_ms, solve_ll_ms,
  speedup_auto_vs_ll = factor_ll / factor_auto,
  res_auto, res_ll

`backend` is a literal "linked-list" or "csc" — the value AUTO
selected internally.

Corpus
------
Sprint 19 SuiteSparse corpus (`nos4`, `bcsstk04`, `bcsstk14`,
`s3rmt3m3`) plus an in-memory `kkt-150` fixture (140×140 tridiagonal
SPD top block + 10×10 zero bottom + identity-pattern coupling on
the first 10 top-block columns; same fixture as
`test_sprint20_integration.c::test_s20_auto_above_threshold_indefinite_kkt_routes_csc`).

Observations
------------
1. **AUTO selected `csc` on every corpus row.**  n >= 100 on all
   five fixtures, so the Day 5 threshold heuristic routes CSC
   uniformly.  Forced LINKED_LIST runs for the left-side comparison.

2. **Residuals match between paths at round-off.**  Every
   (res_auto, res_ll) pair is at 1e-15 or below.  kkt-150 produces
   exact zero residual because the solve reconstructs the all-ones
   RHS to machine precision.

3. **Small-matrix overhead.**  nos4 (n=100), bcsstk04 (n=132), and
   kkt-150 show AUTO slower than forced LINKED_LIST (speedup ≈
   0.46-0.78×).  The CSC dispatch runs a scalar pre-pass to resolve
   BK swaps (`ldlt_csc_eliminate_native` on a heuristic CSC) and
   then attempts the batched supernodal factor — double the numeric
   work on matrices where the batched path's structural advantage
   is small.  This matches the Sprint 18 Cholesky crossover pattern:
   the AUTO threshold targets moderate-to-large matrices where the
   batched kernel's amortisation wins.

4. **Large-matrix win on bcsstk14 (1.10×) and s3rmt3m3 (1.08×).**
   The batched path's `SPARSE_ERR_SINGULAR` trip on these matrices
   (see Day 6 fallback NOTE below) causes Day 5's structural
   fallback to drop through to the scalar CSC factor already
   computed in the pre-pass.  Even with that fallback, AUTO edges
   out forced LINKED_LIST because the CSC scalar kernel is faster
   than the linked-list kernel on these sizes (as Sprint 18/19
   bench_ldlt_csc shows 2-4× native-scalar-CSC speedup over
   linked-list on bcsstk14 / s3rmt3m3).  The dispatch's CSC-path
   overhead (AMD + pre-pass + sparse_permute + sparse_analyze +
   from_sparse_with_analysis + failed batched attempt + writeback)
   narrows the margin here relative to the raw CSC scalar bench.

Day 6 fallback NOTE (bcsstk14 / s3rmt3m3)
-----------------------------------------
During the Day 6 integration work we discovered that the Sprint 19
batched supernodal LDL^T kernel trips `SPARSE_ERR_SINGULAR` on
bcsstk14 (n=1806) and s3rmt3m3 (n=5357) when driven from a
sym_L-preallocated CSC produced by `ldlt_csc_from_sparse_with_analysis`
on an AMD-reordered + BK-pre-permuted matrix.  Sprint 19's
`bench_ldlt_csc --supernodal` on bcsstk14 also exhibits the issue,
producing `res_csc_sn = 1.93e+04` (effectively garbage) — that was
invisible until Sprint 19 PR #27 review added the `res_csc_sn`
column, and the Sprint 20 Day 6 dispatch work surfaced it at the
public API level.

Root cause: on these specific matrices the dense BK factor inside
`ldlt_csc_supernode_eliminate_diag` trips the singularity threshold
(`ldlt_dense_factor` returning SPARSE_ERR_SINGULAR) despite the
scalar native kernel factoring the same matrix without trouble.
Numerical drift between the scalar pre-pass's L values and the
batched kernel's supernode-local diagonal scaling appears to be
the culprit.

Mitigation in Day 5's CSC dispatch: the structural fallback
(originally only catching `SPARSE_ERR_BADARG` from the pivot-
stability check) was widened on Day 6 to catch any batched failure.
F_pre holds a valid scalar factor by the time the batched attempt
runs, so ANY non-OK batched result falls back to F_pre's factor.
`used_csc_path` stays at 1 — the CSC kernel chain handled the
factor end-to-end.

Follow-up for a later sprint: investigate the exact BK numerical-
drift condition on bcsstk14 / s3rmt3m3 and either tighten
`ldlt_dense_factor`'s tolerance policy, scale the dense diagonal
relative to the full matrix norm, or add a dense-kernel retry with
the scalar kernel's pivot decisions.  Tracked in the Sprint 20
retrospective (Day 14).
